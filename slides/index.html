The Challenge

How to reconstruct activity spaces from sampled locations?

Brownian Bridges

The Brownian bridge movement model (Bullard 1999) is based on the probability distribution of a particle following a Brownian random walk between two known locations.

Brownian bridge between two sampled locations. Contour lines show probability of presence during the sampled time interval.

Individual Brownian bridges between all sampled locations. Contour lines show probability of presence during each time interval between the observed points.

Brownian bridge movement model for the full sample. Contour lines show integrated probability of presence during entire 24-hour sampling period. Model estimated using dynamic approach of Kranstauber et al. (2012).
